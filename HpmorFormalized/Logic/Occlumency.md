In HPMOR Chapter 27, Occlumency is the magical art of shielding your mind from Legilimency (mind-reading). We modeled this as a cryptographic indistinguishability problem: the Occlumens must present a fake mental state that a Legilimens cannot distinguish from a genuine one. We proved that perfect Occlumency (fooling any possible Legilimens) requires the fake distribution to be identical to the real distribution — you must perfectly simulate a genuine mind to fake one, which creates a paradox: you need to know exactly what a genuine mind looks like. The weaker version (fooling only computationally limited Legilimens) is more plausible but requires that mind-reading has computational limits — an assumption HPMOR never makes. The story treats Occlumency as "clearing your mind," but the math says it's constructing a simulation.
