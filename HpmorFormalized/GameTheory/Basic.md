Throughout HPMOR, Harry uses game theory in negotiations -- with Draco Malfoy, in the Azkaban arc, and in the final confrontation with Voldemort. We modeled two-player games using payoff matrices (each player picks a strategy, and the pair of choices determines both players' payoffs) and defined Nash equilibrium: a pair of strategies where neither player can do better by switching on their own. We also set up the Nash bargaining problem, which asks how two players should split a surplus fairly, and proved the simple starting fact that the bargaining objective equals zero at the disagreement point (the fallback outcome if no deal is reached). This module is currently at Tier 1-2: it lays down definitions and proves basic properties that follow directly from those definitions, but does not yet contain substantive theorems. The big goals listed for future work -- proving that Nash equilibria always exist in mixed strategies, formalizing subgame-perfect equilibrium for Voldemort's ultimatum, and modeling coalition formation -- would bring it to a higher tier. As it stands, this module is scaffolding for those deeper results rather than a meaningful formalization on its own.
