In HPMOR Chapters 22-24, Harry and Draco negotiate, and the story implies that rational Bayesian agents who share their reasoning should eventually agree. We formalized Aumann's Agreement Theorem, which makes this precise: if two agents share a common prior and their posteriors are common knowledge, they must be equal. But the theorem requires a single shared prior — and Harry (raised on Muggle science) and Draco (raised on pureblood ideology) have very different priors. We proved a counterexample showing that with different priors, two perfect Bayesians can rationally disagree even with full common knowledge. The hidden assumption HPMOR never mentions is that "rational agents must agree" is only true if they started from the same background model of the world.
