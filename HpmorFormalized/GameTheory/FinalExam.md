In HPMOR Chapters 113-122, Harry faces Voldemort in a final confrontation modeled as an ultimatum game. Voldemort demands Harry surrender his wand and magic or everyone dies. Harry has a hidden option: partial transfiguration. We formalized the game with three strategies (comply, refuse, exploit) and proved that the exploit strategy is optimal whenever Harry believes his success probability exceeds a threshold. With reasonable payoff values, that threshold is about 55% — surprisingly low. Harry didn't need near-certainty that his trick would work, just a better-than-even chance. The specific threshold depends on the payoff values chosen (which are somewhat arbitrary), so the number itself is a modeling artifact, but the structural result — that a threshold exists strictly between 0 and 1 — is robust.
