In HPMOR Chapter 33, Harry argues that repeating the Prisoner's Dilemma changes the equilibrium — cooperation becomes sustainable through iteration. We formalized the folk theorem with the exact discount factor threshold: cooperation via grim trigger is sustainable if and only if the discount factor delta satisfies delta >= (T-R)/(T-P), where T is the temptation payoff, R is the reward for mutual cooperation, and P is the punishment for mutual defection. The surprising finding is that S — the sucker payoff for cooperating when the other person defects — does not appear in this formula at all. How bad it is to be suckered has no effect on whether cooperation is sustainable. Harry could have said this and it would have been a genuinely non-obvious insight.
