In HPMOR Chapters 2-3, Harry sees Professor McGonagall turn into a cat and reasons that even though his prior probability for "magic is real" was tiny, the evidence is so overwhelming that his confidence must shoot up to near certainty. We modeled this using the standard formula from Bayes' theorem: the posterior (updated belief) equals L times the prior divided by (L times the prior plus one minus the prior), where L is the "likelihood ratio" -- a number measuring how much more likely the evidence is if the hypothesis is true. We proved that as L grows toward infinity, the posterior approaches 1, no matter how small the prior was (as long as it was not exactly zero). We also proved that the posterior always increases when L increases, and that it always stays between 0 and 1 as a proper probability should. The formalization confirms Harry's reasoning and makes one thing precise: a prior of exactly zero can never be updated, no matter how strong the evidence -- so a truly closed mind cannot be changed by any amount of data.
