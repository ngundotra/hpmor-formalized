In HPMOR, Time-Turners enforce causal consistency: any time-travel influence must fit into a self-consistent history with no paradoxes. One way to guarantee this is to require the causal structure of events to form a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG), where no chain of cause-and-effect can loop back on itself. We modeled this using a general binary relation on events with an explicit acyclicity condition (no directed cycles through the transitive closure), and proved three key results without any sorry: (1) acyclicity on finite sets implies well-foundedness, enabling induction along causal chains; (2) every finite DAG admits a topological ordering, constructed by counting ancestors; and (3) tree-shaped DAGs always have consistent state assignments, built by well-founded recursion. A genuine finding from formalization: consistent propagation along DAG edges requires each event to have at most one direct cause (a "forest" structure) -- for events with multiple causes, a separate merge function is needed, which is a constraint the HPMOR narrative does not mention.
