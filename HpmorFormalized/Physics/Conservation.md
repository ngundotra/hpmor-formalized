In HPMOR Chapter 2, Harry objects that Transfiguration violates conservation of energy, which is implied by time-translation symmetry via Noether's theorem. We formalized a simplified version of this argument using discrete dynamical systems and proved Harry is right — magic that doesn't preserve energy can't commute with the symmetry that generates energy conservation. But we also proved something Harry never considers: magic breaking one conservation law doesn't break all of them. We constructed a concrete system where "magic" violates energy conservation while preserving a different conserved quantity under a different symmetry. "Magic violates physics" is less precise than "magic violates one specific symmetry while possibly respecting others."
